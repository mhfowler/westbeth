"interactive art and embodiment" by Nathaniel Stern suggests that Claude Shannon's information theory was in a sense where philosophy went all wrong — where corporeality was separated from information (when this separation could never truly be complete).

I also read an article by Mitchel Waldrop where Claude Shannon is revered.

Both accounts agree that the quantification of information into a theory — perhaps first hinted at in Claude's Master's thesis "A Symbolic Analysis of Relay and Switching Circuits" — was a pivotal moment.

What culture was motivating the thinkers at Bell Lab's during the time that these early ideas were synthesized — maybe by understanding the place these ideas came from we can see again what has now become invisible through its ubiquity (what is computation).
